Safety Assessment Definition

10. Violence, including domestic or family violence, is suspected in the home AND poses a threat to the life or physical safety of the alleged victim/client.


Suspicion of violence may be based on indicators such as the following.

  • The alleged victim/client was previously injured in a violent incident involving the alleged perpetrator or other persons who have access to the alleged victim/client.
  • The alleged victim/client is being isolated and/or denied access to emergency services (e.g., is not being allowed to access a telephone or to leave the home).
  • The alleged victim/client exhibits severe emotional distress (e.g., fear, agitation, confusion, severe depression, insomnia).
  • The alleged victim/client's behavior increases the risk of injury.
  • Use of guns, knives, or other instruments in a violent, threatening, and/or intimidating manner.
  • Evidence of property damage resulting from violence.
  • History of violence at the residence

AND these actions or conditions pose a threat to life or physical safety of the alleged victim/client.
