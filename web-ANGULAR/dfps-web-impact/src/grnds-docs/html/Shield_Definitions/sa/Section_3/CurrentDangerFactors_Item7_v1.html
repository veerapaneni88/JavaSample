Safety Assessment Definition

7. The alleged victim/client's cognitive status places him/her at imminent risk to life or physical safety.


The alleged victim/client shows signs of impaired cognitive functioning, such as evidence of impaired reasoning or problem solving, disorientation, disorganized thoughts, unusually restless behavior, and/or substantial memory impairments. Observation indicates that the alleged victim/client is unable to complete some or all daily tasks or that the alleged victim/client overestimates his/her ability to carry out plans expressed to the caseworker or others.

Examples of imminent risk to life or physical safety include but are not limited to the following.
  • Acts of aggression, which can include physical assault of others in the home or throwing and breaking objects.
  • Repeated injuries due to wandering, dangerous behavior, etc.
  • Consistent inability to meet basic needs to the extent that health is endangered (e.g., malnourishment, dehydration, eating rotten food, leaving the stove on and unattended, escalating health concerns due to inability to comply with medication, etc.).

AND these actions or conditions pose a threat to life or physical safety of the alleged victim/client.
