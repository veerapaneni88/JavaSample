Safety Assessment Definition

6. The alleged victim/client's immediate needs for mental health care are not met AND failure to meet these needs could result in a threat to life or physical safety for the alleged victim/client or others.


  • The alleged victim/client is either refusing or unable to seek treatment for his/her immediate, chronic, and/or dangerous mental health condition(s) or to follow prescribed treatment for such conditions.
  • The alleged victim/client is experiencing extreme fear, agitation, or delusions.
  • The alleged victim/client is suicidal/homicidal and no protective action has been taken.

AND failure to meet these needs could result in a threat to life or physical safety of the alleged victim/client or others.

Actions that may indicate a threat to life or physical safety to the alleged victim/client or others include the following.
  • Acts of aggression, which can include physical assault of others in the home, throwing and breaking objects, and verbal threats that the alleged victim/client could possibly carry out
  • Assuming the alleged victim/client has the ability to interact, severe withdrawal as indicated by prolonged lack of interest in communicating with others, lack of participation in normal activities involving others, lack of interest in meals, etc.
  • Consistent inability to perform basic personal care to the extent that health is endangered (e.g., malnourishment, dehydration, sunstroke due to inappropriate clothing, escalating health concerns due to inability to comply with medication, etc.).
