Safety Assessment Definition

11. A caretaker is unwilling or unable to protect the alleged victim/client from harm by others AND there is a threat to the life or physical safety of the alleged victim/client.


Indicators of failure to protect may include the following.

  • A caretaker does not provide the supervision necessary to protect the alleged victim/client from harm by others.
  • An individual with known violent criminal behavior/history resides in the home, or a caretaker allows him/her access to the alleged victim/client.
  • A caretaker repeatedly exposes the alleged victim/client to potentially dangerous or harmful people who frequent the home.
  • A caretaker suspects or is aware that the alleged victim/client is being harmed by another adult in the home and takes no action.

AND there is a threat to the life or physical safety of the alleged victim/client.
