Safety Assessment Definition

9. Sexual abuse is suspected, and circumstances suggest that the alleged victim/client's safety is of current concern.


Suspicion of sexual abuse may be based on indicators such as the following.

  • The alleged victim/client discloses sexual abuse either verbally or behaviorally (e.g., unexplained withdrawal from normal activities, sudden change in affect, unusual depression, sexualized behavior, fearfulness, or indirect disclosures).
  • Medical findings consistent with sexual contact or sexually transmitted disease, or physical evidence/injury indicating possible sexual abuse.
  • The alleged perpetrator or others in the household have been accused of, investigated for, or convicted of sexual assault.
  • Using the alleged victim/client for the sexual gratification of others. This includes use of the alleged victim/client for the purposes of prostitution and other forms of sexual gratification, such as posting explicit photos of the alleged victim/client on the Internet or allowing the alleged victim/client to be viewed by others for sexual gratification.
  • The alleged perpetrator has unsupervised access to the alleged victim/client.
  • The alleged perpetrator is in a position of authority or power over the alleged victim/client.


AND the alleged victim/client's safety from sexual abuse is of current concern.
