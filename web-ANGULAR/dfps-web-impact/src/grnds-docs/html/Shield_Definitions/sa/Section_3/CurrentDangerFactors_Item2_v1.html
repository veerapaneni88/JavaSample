Safety Assessment Definition

2. The physical living conditions are hazardous and currently threatening the health and/or safety of the alleged victim/client.


A hazardous living situation or physical environment jeopardizes the minimum health and safety of an alleged victim/client. Examples include but are not limited to the following.

  • Lack of access to operational utilities or facilities that presents imminent risk to alleged victim/client's health, safety, or well-being. For example:
    • Nonexistent or inoperable heating/cooling devices AND current weather conditions require heating/cooling for the preservation of health;
    • Nonexistent or inoperable toileting or bathing facilities; or
    • Lack of electricity that makes it impossible to operate oxygen equipment or other assistive devices.

  • Damage to the structure that poses an immediate safety or health hazard, and the alleged victim/client is unable to address this or remove self from the home. For example:
    • Broken or missing windows or doors that leave the alleged victim/client exposed to the elements and/or unprotected from intruders;
    • Collapsed roof or flooring; or
    • Exposed electrical wires that pose a risk of fire or electrocution.

  • Environmental conditions that threaten health and/or safety, and the alleged victim/client is unable to remove him/herself from the environment. For example:
    • The presence of dangerous objects or substances (e.g., methamphetamine [meth] labs);
    • Downed power lines on or near the alleged victim/client's property or a tree has fallen on the home;
    • Debris that blocks access to or egress from the home or movement within the home;
    • Flooding or extreme weather conditions;
    • Gas/oil leak(s) or toxic fumes;
    • Excessive garbage, spoiled food, or human or animal waste that has already affected or is likely to affect the health of the alleged victim/client, and he/she is unable to address this or remove him/herself from the environment.

  • A lack of shelter or impending lack of shelter (e.g., eviction, foreclosure) that presents an imminent threat of serious harm.


  • Animals, pests, vermin, or insects in the home that are known or suspected to affect the alleged victim/client's health or safety, and the alleged victim/client is unable or unwilling to address this or remove him/herself from the environment.
