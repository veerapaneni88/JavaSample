Safety Assessment Definition

5. The alleged victim/client's immediate needs for physical care and/or medical care are not met AND failure to meet these needs could result in a threat to life or physical safety.


Examples of unmet current needs for physical care and/or medical care include but are not limited to the following.

  • Minimal nutritional needs of the alleged victim/client are not met, or the alleged victim/client appears malnourished, dehydrated, and/or emaciated.


  • The alleged victim/client is either refusing or unable to seek treatment for his/her immediate, chronic, and/or dangerous medical condition(s) or to follow prescribed treatment. Include alleged victims/clients who refuse transport to the hospital or leave facilities against medical advice.


  • The alleged victim/client lacks life-sustaining medication and/or medical equipment (e.g., oxygen nebulizer)


  • The alleged victim/client is without a caretaker for inappropriate periods of time and is unable to provide minimal care for him/herself.


  • The alleged victim/client is not appropriately dressed for environmental conditions.

AND failure to meet these needs could result in a threat to life or physical safety.
