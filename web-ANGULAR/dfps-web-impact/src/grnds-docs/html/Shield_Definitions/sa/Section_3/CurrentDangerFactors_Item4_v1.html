Safety Assessment Definition

4. The alleged victim/client and/or caretaker's explanation for an observed injury to the alleged victim/client is questionable or inconsistent with the type of injury AND circumstances indicate that the alleged victim/client is facing imminent threat to life or physical safety.


  • The alleged victim/client and/or caretaker deny the injury or attribute it to accidental causes. Medical evaluation indicates that the injury is not consistent with the explanation given by the alleged victim/client and/or caretaker.


  • The alleged victim/client and/or caretaker's explanation for the injury is inconsistent with the type of injury (e.g., the alleged victim/client has a mark on the face consistent with the shape of a hand, but the alleged victim/client or caretaker claims that he/she fell).


  • The alleged victim/client has experienced a significant injury and the alleged victim/client and/or caretaker's description of the injury or cause of the injury minimizes the extent of harm to the alleged victim/client.


  • Factors to consider include the alleged victim/client's ability to protect him/herself, location of injury, special needs of the alleged victim/client, or chronic nature of injuries.

AND circumstances indicate that the alleged victim/client is in imminent threat to life or physical safety.
