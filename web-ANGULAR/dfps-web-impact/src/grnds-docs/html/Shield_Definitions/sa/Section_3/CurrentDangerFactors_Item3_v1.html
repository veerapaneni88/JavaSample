Safety Assessment Definition

3. The alleged victim/client experienced serious physical injury or a plausible threat to his/her life or physical safety, as indicated by the following.


  • Injury or abuse to the alleged victim/client other than accidental. The alleged victim/client sustained a non-accidental physical injury by another person. Injury includes but is not limited to bone fractures, bruises, dislocations, sprains, internal injuries, poisoning, burns, scalds, or severe cuts.


  • Threat to cause harm or retaliate against the alleged victim/client. A threat of action exists that would result in serious harm, or there is a threat to retaliate against the alleged victim/client.


  • Use of unauthorized restraint. Unauthorized restraints (physical, chemical, or mechanical) were used on the alleged victim/client, medications not prescribed for the alleged victim/client were provided to him/her, or the alleged victim/client was intentionally and inappropriately administered prescribed or over-the-counter medications.


  • Caretaker who voices concern that he/she will maltreat the alleged victim/client. Caretaker expresses frustration about his/her ability to handle the alleged victim/client without losing his/her temper and maltreating the alleged victim/client, and/or caretaker expresses the need for immediate relief from caring for the alleged victim/client.
