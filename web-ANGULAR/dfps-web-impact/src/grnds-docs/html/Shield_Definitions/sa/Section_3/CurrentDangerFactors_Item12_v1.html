Safety Assessment Definition

12. In the absence of other danger factors, there is a history of abuse, neglect (including self-neglect), or exploitation indicating that there is a threat to the life or physical safety of the alleged victim/client.


This item may only be selected if no other danger factors have been identified.

Prior investigation factors to be considered include but are not limited to the following.

  • Seriousness and/or patterns of allegations.
  • Prior injury and/or medical crisis.
  • The alleged victim/client's safety is of current concern because the caretaker has an APS history of abuse, neglect, or exploitation as a perpetrator.
  • Prior placement, voluntary or involuntary, was necessary for the safety of the alleged victim/client.

AND the alleged victim/client is at imminent risk of further self-neglect or neglect, physical abuse, or exploitation by another.
