Safety Assessment Definition

1. Assessment of the alleged victim/client's situation is impeded AND there is reason to believe that the alleged victim/client is facing imminent threat to life or physical safety.


This danger factor is present if two conditions are met: impeded assessment and imminent serious harm.

Examples of impeding assessment include but are not limited to the following.

  • The alleged victim/client does not allow the caseworker into the home or does not allow the caseworker to observe his/her living conditions.
  • Caretaker or alleged perpetrator does not allow the alleged victim/client to be interviewed alone.
  • Caretaker or alleged perpetrator refuses access to the alleged victim/client or cannot/will not provide the alleged victim/client's location.
  • Caretaker or alleged perpetrator keeps the alleged victim/client isolated/restrained from friends, neighbors, family members, or other people.
  • Caretaker or alleged perpetrator exerts undue influence to coach or coerce the alleged victim/client in an effort to hinder the investigation or assessment.

AND there is reason to believe that the alleged victim/client is facing imminent threat to life or physical safety.
