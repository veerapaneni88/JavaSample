Immediate Interventions Instructions and Definitions

Immediate interventions are actions recommended specifically to mitigate any current danger factors identified during Case Initiation or during Face to Face Contact. They should address current considerations for safety rather than long-term changes. Immediate interventions should be implemented in accordance with state and local policies and procedures. Mark all interventions recommended by the caseworker to mitigate identified current danger factors, then indicate whether the alleged victim/client accepts the intervention(s).

Instructions

  • Describe the actions recommended by the caseworker to mitigate identified current danger factors.
  • Indicate whether the alleged victim/client accepts the intervention(s)
  • More than one safety intervention type may be recommended for one danger factor, and service acceptance or refusal must be indicated for each intervention.
  • When multiple dangers are present, complete a row for each.
  • When selecting actions for recommendation, it is important to begin with the least restrictive interventions that can effectively mitigate current danger factors.

Action Category Definitions

1. Action by family members, neighbors, and/or friends.
This refers to applying the family's own strengths as resources and/or using extended family members, neighbors, or other individuals to mitigate safety concerns. Examples include but are not limited to: engaging a family member, neighbor, and/or friend to provide temporary assistance, shelter, or care to the alleged victim/client.


2. Action by community/governmental agencies or services (not purchased client services).
This includes involving a community- or faith-based organization, tribal council, or another agency in activities to address safety concerns (e.g., immediate psychological or medical evaluation; emergency home health care services or nursing home placement; access to emergency food, shelter, and medication; utility assistance; major clean-up and repair services; public health authority; housing inspectors; animal control). THIS DOES NOT INCLUDE long-term therapy, treatment, referrals, or being put on a waiting list for services.


3. APS-direct provision of services (do not include the investigation or assessment itself).
This refers to actions taken by the caseworker or other APS staff that specifically address one or more current danger factors. Examples include but are not limited to: delivering materials from APS resource rooms, delivering medication or food, and/or calling 911.


4. Legal action
Legal action was taken by law enforcement, APS, or another agency to ensure the alleged victim/client's safety.


5. Placement
The alleged victim/client was removed from the home and placed in a hospital, nursing home, or other alternate care facility.


6. Purchased client services (includes purchase order for placement services)
Any goods or services purchased or pledged using purchased client services funds.


7. Other safety intervention
The alleged victim/client, family, or caseworker identified a unique intervention for an identified safety concern that does not fit within items 1 through 6.

Action Result Definitions

1. Action successful
The indicated action was successful in addressing and mitigating the identified danger factor.


2. Action unsuccessful
The indicated action was not successful at addressing or mitigating the identified danger factor.


3. Client refused services/withdrew
The alleged victim/client was offered services to address the identified danger factor but refused services OR he/she initially agreed to services but withdrew before completing services or using them to their fullest intended extent.


4. Caretaker refused services/withdrew
The caretaker may also refuse services. Refusal scenarios include:
  • When the caretaker has been offered services to help him/her mitigate the alleged victim/client's current danger factors
  • When the caretaker has been offered services to address the role he/she may have had in creating the danger factors, including as an alleged perpetrator
Select the 'Caretaker refused services/withdrew' action result in the refusal scenarios if the caretaker refused services OR the caretaker initially agreed to services but withdrew before completing services or using them to their fullest intended extent. Also select the 'Caretaker refused services/withdrew' action result when the caretaker is refusing to participate in protective actions being offered to the client.


5. No services available
Client is in need of services to address the current identified danger factor, but no services are available.

Outcome Definitions

1. N/A - Client died

2. No - Client has refused all actions/withdrew
Client has refused all actions and services.


3. No - Efforts/services exhausted or insufficient
The indicated danger factor was not resolved by the associated action(s) because available services were insufficient to mitigate the indicated danger factor. Do not mark this item if the alleged victim/client refused services. Only mark this item if the alleged victim/client engaged in services, these services were not successful in addressing the danger factor, and there are no remaining services that could be provided to the alleged victim/client.


4. No - Moved/unable to locate
This outcome is used when the alleged victim moves to another state, or the APS specialist does not know the alleged victim's whereabouts and is therefore unable to make contact with the alleged victim.


5. Yes - Problem resolved
The indicated danger factor was resolved by the associated action(s).
