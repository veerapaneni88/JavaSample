Safety Assessment Definition

Lack of food, life-sustaining medications, or utilities.


The alleged victim's basic needs will go unmet before the first face-to-face contact required by the assigned priority. Examples include but are not limited to: alleged victim lacks food or utilities which will threaten life or health or alleged victim is believed to be without life-sustaining medications, medical treatment, or medical equipment (e.g., oxygen equipment, Hoyer lift, etc.).
