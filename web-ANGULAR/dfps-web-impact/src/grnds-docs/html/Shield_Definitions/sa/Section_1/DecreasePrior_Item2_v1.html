Safety Assessment Definition

The alleged victim will be safe for a longer period than indicated by the assigned priority in the intake report.


Examples of new information that may indicate a less urgent response include but are not limited to: information that an alleged perpetrator no longer has access to the alleged victim (e.g., has left the state, has been incarcerated, has died) or information that the alleged victim has left a dangerous living arrangement and will remain safe for a period through the new priority deadline.
