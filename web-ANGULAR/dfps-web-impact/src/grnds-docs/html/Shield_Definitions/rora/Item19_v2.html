Risk of Recidivism Assessment Definition

19. Primary caretaker has realistic expectations of the client.


  • Not applicable-no caretaker.
  • Yes, realistic expectations: The primary caretaker demonstrates realistic expectations of client.
  • No, unrealistic expectations: The primary caretaker has demonstrated unrealistic expectations of the client, either in the past or currently. These unrealistic expectations may be related to over- or underestimating the client's abilities, as evidenced by the following.

  • Client is expected to behave or perform in ways that are unreasonable given the client's physical and/or mental/cognitive capabilities.


  • Client may not be allowed to engage in self-care activities even though he/she has the ability to complete these tasks.


    • Examples include but are not limited to the following.

  • Client has physical limitations and is expected to move between rooms independently or more quickly than his/her condition allows


  • Client has diagnosed dementia and is expected to remember instructions for taking medication.
