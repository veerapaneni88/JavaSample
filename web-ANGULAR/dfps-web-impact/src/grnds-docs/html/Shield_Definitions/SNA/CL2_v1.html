Strengths and Needs Assessment Definition

CL2. Physical Mobility


  1. Client has no mobility problems OR mobility problems are adequately addressed through current services.
    • Client has no physical mobility problems.
    • Client has mobility problems but is able to move about the home and community independently with or without use of assistive devices.
    • Client has severe mobility problems but basic mobility needs to preserve health and safety are currently met.

  2. Client has mobility problems that interfere with the ability to meet basic needs.
    Client has mobility problems to the extent that he/she is unable to complete some daily activities AND:
    • Alternative provisions (e.g., use of adaptive devices or assistance from others) have not been made; OR
    • Alternative provisions have been made but are insufficient (e.g., client has a walker but is unable to use it to move about the home or community; client has assistance but it is not sufficient to meet mobility needs).

  3. Client is unable to meet basic needs due to mobility problems.
    Client has mobility problems to the extent that the client is unable to complete most/all daily activities AND:
    • Client is completely dependent on others for movement and is not receiving services; OR
    • Client lacks access to adaptive devices or mobility assistance from others, and this lack threatens the client's health or safety.
