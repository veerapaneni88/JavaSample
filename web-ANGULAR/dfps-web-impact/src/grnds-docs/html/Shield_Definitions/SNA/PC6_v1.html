Strengths and Needs Assessment Definition

PC6. Substance Use (Includes alcohol; prescription, over-the-counter or other drugs)


  1. Primary caretaker does not use substances OR substance use does not interfere with ability to provide care.
    • Primary caretaker does not demonstrate any indication of current substance use; uses alcohol occasionally, but use is not problematic; and takes medications as prescribed.
    • Primary caretaker has a history of treated substance use disorder; he/she is active in recovery.

  2. Primary caretaker's substance use somewhat impedes ability to provide care.
    • Primary caretaker demonstrates periodic abuse of alcohol, other drugs, or medications to the extent that there is some concern about the primary caretaker being able to meet the client's needs.
    • Primary caretaker continues to use alcohol, other drugs, or medications despite negative consequences to the ability to provide quality care, to maintain good physical and mental health, and to maintain coping skills.
    • Primary caretaker needs and wants help to address issues related to substance use.

  3. Primary caretaker's substance use interferes with ability to provide care.
    • Primary caretaker demonstrates active substance abuse.
    • Primary caretaker demonstrates that his/her substance use results in behaviors that interfere with his/her ability to meet the client's needs as well as his/her own needs.
    • Primary caretaker has major health and/or safety concerns due to substance use, such as frequent blackouts, hospitalizations or arrests for driving under the influence.
    • Primary caretaker uses the client's medication for his/her own personal use or sale.
    • Primary caretaker needs and refuses extensive help to address issues related to substance use.
