Strengths and Needs Assessment Definition

PC3. Caretaking Knowledge/Skills


  1. Primary caretaker has sufficient knowledge/skills.
    • Primary caretaker demonstrates sufficient knowledge of the client's condition and/or the expected progression of the client's condition, consistent with providing adequate care for the client's needs.
    • Primary caretaker has sufficient skills to complete necessary and appropriate care (e.g., administering medications, assisting with transferring, maintaining routines).

  2. Some concerns related to caretaker's knowledge/skills.
    Primary caretaker lacks the knowledge or skills necessary to meet some of the client's needs and/or lacks knowledge required to adapt to a condition that is changing/challenging (e.g., advancing dementia).

  3. Significant concerns related to caretaker's knowledge/skills.
    Primary caretaker lacks the knowledge or skills necessary to meet most or all of the client's current needs (e.g., administering medications, assisting with transferring, maintaining routines).
