Strengths and Needs Assessment Definition

CL6. Housing/Physical Environment


  1. Adequate housing that meets basic needs for health and safety.
    • Housing conditions such as heat, electrical service, and plumbing are adequate for safe and healthy living.
    • Furnishings and housekeeping are adequate for safe and healthy living.

  2. Some minor concerns related to health and safety of current housing.
    • Housing conditions are present that require repair or replacement but are not of immediate health or safety concern. Examples include a lack of utilities when they are not required for health or safety, the need for minor structural repairs, cleaning, or repair or replacement of an appliance.
    • Some hoarding behavior is noted (e.g., newspapers, food), but not to the level of causing a fire hazard or impeding the client's ability to move about the home.

  3. Significant concerns related to health and safety of current housing.
    • Housing conditions result in threats to health and safety, such as lack of utilities when they are required for health or safety, severely exacerbated asthma due to smoke exposure, multiple bites from pest infestation, etc.
    • Severe hoarding behavior (e.g., animals, newspapers, food) is present, causing a fire hazard or impeding the client's ability to move about the home.
    • Housing has been condemned and/or eviction is likely or imminent.
