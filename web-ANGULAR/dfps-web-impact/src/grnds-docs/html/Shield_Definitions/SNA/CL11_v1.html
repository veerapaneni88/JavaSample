Strengths and Needs Assessment Definition

CL11. Ongoing Relationships


An ongoing relationship is a personal relationship that includes frequent and regular interaction, a reasonable assumption that the interaction will continue, and an establishment of trust, beyond a commercial or contractual agreement (TAC 705.1001). Include all persons with whom the client has an ongoing relationship; this item is not restricted to the alleged perpetrator/perpetrator.

  1. Generally supportive relationships.
    • Client's relationships are stable and supportive with no conflict.
    • There is minor discord, which is nonviolent and non-threatening.

  2. Disruptive relationships.
    • Nonviolent or rarely occurring violent outbursts by client or others, but no physical injuries or law enforcement intervention.
    • Client has consistent, disruptive negative interactions.
    • Occasional threatening or intimidating behavior by client or others.

  3. Extremely problematic relationships.
    • Violence on multiple occasions by client or others, which may have resulted in physical injury or law enforcement intervention.
    • Emotional distress is present that impacts health and/or safety.
