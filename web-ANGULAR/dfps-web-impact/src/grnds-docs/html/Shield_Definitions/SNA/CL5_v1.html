Strengths and Needs Assessment Definition

CL5. Substance Use (Includes alcohol and prescription, over-the-counter, or other drugs.)


  1. No substance use, or substance use has no noticeable adverse effects on health, safety, or daily activities.
    • No indication of current substance use; client uses alcohol occasionally, but use is not problematic; takes medications as prescribed.
    • Client has a history of treated substance disorder; he/she is active in recovery.

  2. Substance use interferes with some daily activities and may affect health and/or safety.
    • Client periodically abuses alcohol, other drugs, or medications to the extent that some daily activities are affected.
    • Client continues to use despite negative consequences in some areas, such as family, social, health, legal, or financial; some daily activities are negatively affected.
    • Client needs and wants help to address issues related to substance use.

  3. Substance use interferes with most or all daily activities and affects health and/or safety.
    • Client actively abuses substances.
    • Client's abuse of alcohol or other drugs results in behaviors that interfere with the ability to meet basic needs; most or all daily activities are negatively affected.
    • There are major health and/or safety concerns due to client's substance use, such as frequent blackouts, hospitalizations, or arrests for driving while under the influence.
    • Client needs and refuses extensive help to address issues related to substance use.
