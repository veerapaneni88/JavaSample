Strengths and Needs Assessment Definition

CL4. Mental Health/Coping Skills (Mental health/coping skills refers to psychological states and emotional and behavioral adjustment.)


  1. Adequate coping skills; client is able to manage mental health problems.
    • Client may have a history of mental health problems but is fully compliant with medication and/or counseling as needed, and symptoms of mental health issues do not interfere with daily functioning.
    • Client demonstrates the ability to problem-solve.

  2. Moderate symptoms that interfere with the performance of some daily activities.
    • Client may have moderate mental health problems and is not fully compliant with medication and/or counseling as needed OR current treatment is insufficient to prevent negative impact on daily activities.
    • Client may have moderate mental health problems that affect one or two daily activities.
    • Client demonstrates episodes of frustration, fatigue, or anger related to changing needs.
    • Client expresses periodic fear/anxiety related to his/her ability to meet basic needs throughout the course of the client's condition.

  3. Chronic/severe symptoms that interfere with the performance of most or all daily activities.
    • Client demonstrates signs of a severe mental health condition and has no relationship with a mental health provider.
    • Client has a diagnosed severe mental illness and is noncompliant with prescribed medication and/or counseling.
    • Client demonstrates an inability to perform most daily activities due to the client's mental health condition.
    • Client demonstrates consistent frustration, fatigue, or anger related to changing needs.
    • Client expresses chronic fear/anxiety related to ability to meet basic needs throughout the course of his/her condition.
