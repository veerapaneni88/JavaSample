Strengths and Needs Assessment Definition

PC4. Physical Health


  1. Primary caretaker's physical health does not interfere with ability to provide care.
    • Primary caretaker does not have any physical health conditions or concerns that impact his/her ability to meet the client's needs.
    • Primary caretaker demonstrates the ability to care for his/her own physical health needs, such as medication or routine medical care.

  2. Primary caretaker's physical health occasionally interferes with ability to provide care.
    • Primary caretaker has a physical health problem or a chronic physical condition that occasionally causes difficulty with his/her ability to provide necessary care.
    • Primary caretaker has episodic physical health issues that occasionally interfere with his/her ability to provide necessary care.

  3. Primary caretaker's physical health interferes with ability to provide care.
    • Primary caretaker has an uncontrollable or poorly controlled chronic illness that interferes with his/her ability to provide necessary care.
    • Primary caretaker demonstrates a recent deterioration in physical health to the point that he/she has significant difficulty providing necessary care.
