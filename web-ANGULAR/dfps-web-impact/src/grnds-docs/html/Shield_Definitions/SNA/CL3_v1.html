Strengths and Needs Assessment Definition

CL3. Cognitive Functioning (Cognitive functioning refers to performance in mental processes such as thinking, understanding, and remembering.)


  1. Good cognitive functioning OR needs related to cognitive function are already met.
    • Client may have mild to moderate cognitive impairment but demonstrates ability to reason and problem-solve.
    • Client has cognitive impairment or limitations, but adequate services to address basic needs for health and safety are already being provided and will continue to be provided without further APS intervention.

  2. Some concerns related to cognitive functioning.
    • Client may have moderate cognitive impairment AND does not have sufficient services in place to maintain health and safety.
    • Client demonstrates impaired reasoning and is unable to problem-solve to the extent that some daily activities are affected AND does not have services in place to maintain health and safety.

  3. Significant concerns related to cognitive functioning.
    • Client has profound cognitive impairment that affects his/her ability to reason and make informed decisions AND does not have sufficient services in place to maintain health and safety.
    • Client demonstrates confusion and is unable to problem-solve to the extent that most daily activities are affected.
    • Client has a history of wandering due to disorientation and does not have adequate services in place to maintain his/her health and safety.
